# Seeding Process — Staff Notes

## STEP 1 - Determine the overall theme
**Vehicle**: email discussions
- Staffers come up with ideas for themes and narrow it down to a final choice (e.g., collectibles, Tolkien locations, movie scenes, characters, etc.)
- Discussions are very organic and fun, and ideas build on other ideas until everyone is happy with the theme and brackets
- Except for this year, the four brackets are decided as part of this process
- Example (from 2020): Overall theme = favorite movie scenes. Brackets = tearjerkers, laughter, scenic, drama/action

## STEP 2 - Gather examples
**Vehicle**: email discussions
- Staffers volunteer suggestions of things to include for the staff to vote on in order to arrive at the final MMM items
- Staffers usually offer suggestions by bracket

**Need to know**: the number of overall suggestions, and suggestions by bracket, always vary from year-to-year. To narrow a bracket down to 16 items, some brackets may start with only 17-18 suggestions, some may have 30 or more

## STEP 3 - Gather/document suggestions
**Vehicle**: 'private' Google spreadsheet
- a volunteer creates a Google sheet (I just copy/change the sheet from the previous year). Suggestions are listed in rows down the first column. A separate field defines the bracket for each suggestion. (see examples I'll provide)
- Staff names are listed across the top of columns
- voting instructions are added at the top of the sheet, and formulas to tally the votes are added at the bottom of each bracket
- when staff suggestions have all been entered, the volunteer shares the link with staff making it 'live' for voting

**Need to know 1**: in the past we've left an extra few columns so staff who weren't listed can add their names and vote. Likewise, a few rows were left at the bottom of each bracket so staffers could enter last-minute finds.

**Need to know 2**: this is the only year we haven't decided on brackets before the spreadsheet was created, and before voting. Therefore, I would err on the side of designing things to cater to previous year formats. The odds of this happening again are very low.

## STEP 4 - Staff voting
**Vehicle**: 'shared' Google spreadsheet
- historic voting guidelines were per bracket and were very simple and easy. Example:
  - Bracket 1
  - 4 4-point votes (favorites)
  - 4 3-pont votes (next favs)
  - 4 2-point votes (next favs)
  - 4 1-point votes (bottom favs)
  - 16 total votes, move on to the next bracket...
- at this stage, with staffers taking a good, long look at everything, changes sometimes occur if items have been duplicated, belong in a different bracket, etc.

**Need to know**: the only reason the voting is ranked at all is to make it easier to break ties regarding what gets into the final 16 for each bracket. Otherwise, it could be made to be even simpler where everyone just puts a 1 next to all 16 of their favorite items.

**Suggestion**: I strongly urge voting to be as simple as possible for the sake of the staff. This is supposed to be a fun exercise where we all get to participate. Complicating it can take some of the fun out of it.

## STEP 5 - Communicate final bracket items ('winners') to Staff and Chris
**Vehicle**: email from the volunteer organizer
- final winners are emailed/announced to staff, and provided to Chris to set up the graphics (I think this, especially, is where your current work is going to shine, Justin)

## A note on voting with no brackets (2026 only)
See below how the voting is different this year. I don't recommend duplicating this for the new program as it won't apply to future years.

(Don't do this or you'll likely have to change it next year)
- 16 votes of 4 (favorite items) = 64
- 16 votes of 3 (next favorite items) = 48
- 16 votes of 2 (next favorite items) = 32
- 16 votes of 1 (bottom fav items) = 16
- Total 'points' = 160

This is actually overly complicated which is my bad. The simplest way to do it would have been to make it more like past years where *votes* would be totaled by 4-point votes, 3-point votes, etc. to equal 64 total votes.
